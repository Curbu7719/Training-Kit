# Using AI Safely — for Everyone

You don't have to build AI to use it well. Most of us will *use* the company's AI tools and agents in everyday work — drafting, summarising, answering, deciding faster. This module is the short, shared set of habits that let you get that help **without** creating a security, privacy, or quality problem. No coding needed.

**1. AI can be confidently wrong.** A model predicts likely text; it does not "know" the truth. It can invent a fact, a policy, a number, or a source that looks right but is not (a **hallucination**), and it only knows things up to its training date (a **knowledge cutoff**). So treat every answer as a **draft to check**, not as the final word. The moment you act on it, you own it — not the AI.

**2. What you send leaves your hands.** Whatever you paste into a prompt goes to the tool. Never paste secrets, passwords, customer personal data (**PII** — data that identifies a person), or confidential code into an unapproved tool. Use the **company's approved AI platform**, send the least you need, and remove sensitive details first. Ask yourself: "Would I be okay if this showed up in a vendor's logs?" If not, don't paste it.

**3. Verify before it counts.** For low-stakes help (a rough draft, a brainstorm), a quick glance is fine. For anything that ships, decides, or goes to a customer, **check the AI's output against a trusted source** and have a human confirm it. Match the amount of checking to the stakes.

**4. Outside content can carry hidden instructions.** When AI summarises a document, email, or ticket, that text might contain hidden instructions that try to hijack it (**prompt injection**). Be careful acting on a result that came from content you did not write — confirm the action is one you actually asked for.

**5. Know when to escalate.** High-stakes, regulated, or hard-to-undo decisions (money, hiring, legal, production, personal data) need human judgment and the right approvals — not an AI acting alone. When in doubt, ask security or governance.

## How each role uses this

- **Everyone:** Treats AI output as a draft to verify, keeps sensitive data out of prompts, and uses only the approved platform.
- **Project Manager:** Checks AI-drafted plans, updates, and stories against the real facts before sharing them as decisions.
- **Portfolio Manager:** Uses AI to speed up analysis but verifies numbers and claims before they inform a budget or roadmap.
- **Governance:** Sets and models the rules — approved tools, what data may be sent, and when human sign-off is required.
- **Designer:** Treats AI drafts and content as a starting point, checking facts and tone before they reach a user.
- **Release Manager:** Never lets an AI-suggested change ship without the normal human review and approval.
