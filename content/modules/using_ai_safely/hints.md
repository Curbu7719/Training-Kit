# Hints — Using AI Safely

## Alternative phrasings of the core idea

- You can use AI well without building it: treat its answers as drafts, keep sensitive data out, and verify anything that matters.
- The five habits: (1) AI can be confidently wrong, (2) what you send leaves your control, (3) verify before it counts, (4) outside content can hide instructions, (5) escalate high-stakes calls.
- Confidence is not correctness. The moment you act on an AI answer, you own it.

## Hint stack

- **H1 (nudge):** Before you paste, ask "what's actually in this text?" Before you act, ask "did I verify this against something I trust?"
- **H2 (structural):** Split it into *what you send* (no PII/secrets, approved tool), *what you get back* (a draft to check), and *what you do next* (match the check to the stakes; escalate if high-impact).
- **H3 (worked):** AI says the refund window is 30 days → it's a draft → check the policy page → it's 14 → you avoided telling a customer the wrong thing.

## FAQ

- **Is it ever okay to skip checking?** For throwaway, low-stakes help, yes. For anything that ships, decides, or reaches a customer, no.
- **What if I'm not sure whether data is sensitive?** Treat it as sensitive and use the approved platform, or ask governance. Cheaper than a leak.
