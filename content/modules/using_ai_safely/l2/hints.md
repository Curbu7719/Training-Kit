# Hints — Using AI Well (L2)

## Alternative phrasings of the core idea

- Safety at depth is *judgment*: how much to verify, how to spot a wrong answer, what you may paste, and when to escalate.
- Verify in proportion to the stakes; distrust confident specifics with no source; let the data tier (public/internal/confidential) decide what you send; treat AI actions off external content as suspect.
- The human stays accountable — "the AI said so" is never the answer in a review.

## Hint stack

- **H1 (nudge):** Ask "what happens if this is wrong?" The bigger the answer, the harder you check.
- **H2 (structural):** Separate *summarise* (usually safe) from *act* (confirm it's something you asked for), especially when the content came from outside.
- **H3 (worked):** A clean "4.2 hours" headed for a leadership deck → high stakes → trace to source → it was 6.8 → wrong number caught before it drove a decision.

## FAQ

- **How much checking is enough?** Match it to the cost of being wrong. Throwaway: almost none. Decision/customer/irreversible: trace to a source and add a human.
- **What are the signs of a hallucination?** Confident specifics with no traceable source, invented citations/links, or APIs/policies that don't exist.
